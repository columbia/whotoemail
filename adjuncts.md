# Process for Hiring Adjuncts (Columbia CS)

## Deadlines
- **Fall semester:** February  
- **Spring semester:** October  
- **Approval timeline:** ~2–4 weeks  
- Process coordinated by **Stephen A. Edwards**

## Requirements

- **CV required** for approval  
- Must identify a **faculty sponsor**  
- Prior teaching experience preferred  
  - Industry teaching experience is acceptable  
- **Instructor approval is separate from course approval**


## Course Structure Guidelines

### 4000-Level (Undergraduate)
- Example: `COMS 4995`
- ~150 minutes/week  
- Can be 1 or 2 sessions  
- Open to undergraduates  

### 6000-Level (Graduate)
- ~110 minutes/week  
- Can be 1 or 2 sessions  
- Primarily for graduate students  
- Qualified undergraduates may enroll  


## Approval Process

### 1. Initial Proposal
- Sponsor identifies:
  - Instructor  
  - Course idea  
    - New course or existing course  


### 2. Academic Committee (AC) Review
- Votes separately on:
  - Course offering  
  - Instructor recommendation  

- Evaluation criteria:
  - Student demand  
  - Overlap with existing courses  
    - May require coordination with other instructors  
  - Course structure (e.g., project-based vs lecture)  
  - Instructor experience  


### 3. Chair & Dean Approval
- Department Chair makes final departmental decision  
- Then submitted to the **Dean for final approval**



## Post-Approval / Onboarding

Once approved:

1. **Appointment letter issued**  
2. **UNI assigned**  
3. **System + campus access granted**



## Key Considerations

- Courses must demonstrate **clear differentiation** from existing offerings  
- Some overlap is acceptable if courses are **distinct in focus or approach**  
- Project-heavy courses are typically better suited for **4000-level**
